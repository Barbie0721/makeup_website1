💄 Natural School Makeup

Best for: Students, beginners, and anyone who loves a fresh, natural makeup look.

🌼 Step-by-Step Tutorial

🔹 Primer

Use Za 03 Pink Tone-Up Cream to brighten your skin tone and give a healthy, radiant base.

🔹 Base Makeup
  • Apply Clio Cushion Foundation (2-BP) evenly across your face.
  • Set with Canmake Pressed Powder for a smooth, non-sticky finish.
🔹 Lip Care

Prep your lips with Mentholatum Lip Balm for moisture and a better lipstick hold.

🔹 Eyebrows
  • Fill in sparse areas with Kate Eyebrow Powder EX-4
  • Define natural shape using lilybyred Eyebrow Pencil (04 Dark Brown)
🔹 Eye Makeup
  • Apply a base using the Dasique Eyeshadow Palette, and deepen the outer corners.
  • Tightline with lilybyred Eyeliner (02 Brown) for a subtle eye definition.
  • Curl lashes using Muji Eyelash Curler
  • Fix the curl with Maybelline Mascara Primer
🔹 Blush & Contour
  • Use 3CE Blush (#Delectable) on your cheeks in an upward diagonal motion.
  • Contour the cheekbones and nose sides lightly with heme Contour Powder (00)
🔹 Lip Makeup

Finish with I'm Meme Lipstick (02) for a soft, natural lip color.

🎀 Pro Tips

  • Keep the base makeup light and breathable—avoid too much layering.
  • Blush should be applied upward along the apples of the cheeks for a fresh and lifted look.
  • For extra oil control, use a cotton swab with pressed powder on the T-zone.

My Beauty Journey - From Stage Lights to Daily Glow

My serious journey into makeup began when I joined the dance club in college. Every time we had a performance, I had to rely on others to help me with my stage makeup. But before shows, everyone was busy preparing themselves, and I didn't want to keep bothering others. I also feared that if no one had time to help, I would have to go on stage feeling insecure and unprepared. That fear motivated me to start learning makeup seriously — from everyday looks to special performance styles.

I taught myself by watching countless tutorials online, practicing every day. At first, it was frustrating: my makeup never looked quite right, and something always felt off. But little by little, with daily practice and adjustments, I got closer to the looks I envisioned.

Now, my friends often compliment my makeup skills, and many even ask me for tips. I'm especially inspired by Korean beauty trends — I love referencing Korean brands and girl group stage makeup for ideas. More than just a skill, makeup has become a joyful part of my daily life. Depending on my mood or outfit, I enjoy creating different looks to express myself.

Through makeup, I've found confidence, creativity, and a hobby that I truly love. One day, I hope to make it not just a passion, but something I'm truly exceptional at.
